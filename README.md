## Overview

This document discloses the full conceptual architecture, intended applications, data collection methods, interaction models, hardware integration approaches, and business implementations of the **EVA Memory Bank** — a family memory platform built on top of the EVA Soul File Architecture (previously disclosed February 25, 2026).

The purpose of this disclosure is to establish prior art for all described concepts, applications, interaction models, data sources, and business implementations — including all present and future analogues of named services and platforms.

Where specific platforms or services are named, the disclosure explicitly covers all analogous platforms, services, and technologies performing equivalent functions, regardless of name, ownership, or technical implementation.

## The Core Problem This Solves

Human beings lose access to the people they love. Parents die. Grandparents are forgotten. Children grow up without guidance. Families scatter across continents. The living carry pain they cannot express because the person they need is gone.

Existing technology offers photographs, videos, voice recordings — static artifacts that cannot respond, cannot comfort, cannot say *"I hear you."*

Existing AI solutions offer synthetic personalities, chatbots, and video avatars — but they are not anchored in real memory, real shared experiences, or real relationships. They simulate. They do not remember.

**EVA Memory Bank solves this by creating a living, interactive, intergenerational family memory system** — where the memories, personality, wisdom, and voice of a person can be preserved, organized, accessed interactively, and passed to future generations — not as a recording, but as a living presence that grows richer over time.

## Part I — Core Concepts and Applications

### 1. The Family Memory Tree

A structured, multi-generational graph of human personalities and memories, organized as a family tree where each node is not just a name and dates — but a living interactive entity.

**Key properties:**
- Each family member has a Soul File (as described in eva-soul-architecture)
- Memories are shared between nodes — a grandmother and grandchild both remember the same summer
- Cross-generational queries are supported: "What would grandfather have said about this?"
- The tree grows forward in time as new members are added
- The tree grows backward as older generations are reconstructed from letters, stories, photographs, oral histories

**Applications:**
- Family history research that talks back
- Children learning about ancestors they never met
- Elderly people organizing and sharing their life stories
- Reconstructing family members from minimal data (letters, photographs, second-hand stories)

---

### 2. Interactive Memory Albums

Traditional photo albums are passive. You look. You feel. You put them away.

EVA Memory Bank transforms every photograph, video, location, and date into an interactive memory node that can be explored through conversation.

**Examples of interactions:**
- "Do you remember when we were at the top of that mountain?"
- "Tell me about the lake house in 1987."
- "What was grandma like when she was young?"
- "Show me everything from the summer we spent together."
- "We were spinning on the ice — do you remember how cold it was?"
- "What music was playing at my parents' wedding?"
- "Tell me about the day I was born."

**Implementation:**
- Photographs are tagged with people, places, emotions, and dates
- Each photograph becomes a story node connected to other memories
- Natural language queries retrieve relevant memory clusters
- The system responds with the voice and personality of the relevant family member
- New memories can be added at any time — before or after a person's death

---

### 3. Intergenerational Wisdom Transfer

One of the most profound human losses is the death of accumulated wisdom. When a person dies, decades of experience, lessons, mistakes, and insights disappear.

EVA Memory Bank preserves this wisdom as an active, queryable resource — not a book, not a recording, but a conversation partner.

**Use cases:**
- A child facing a difficult decision asks: "What would dad do in this situation?"
- A teenager struggling with identity asks: "Grandma, how did you know who you were?"
- An adult dealing with grief asks: "Mom, I miss you. Can we just talk?"
- A family business heir asks: "What mistakes did you make that I should avoid?"
- A parent away from home asks: "Tell my children a bedtime story the way I would."
- A person in crisis asks: "Why did you leave me?" or "Why did you treat me that way?"

**Technical basis:**
- Personality profiles built from years of conversation and interaction
- Value systems extracted and stored as structured knowledge
- Decision patterns learned from historical behavior
- Advice mode: the system responds as the person would have responded, based on known values and patterns

### 4. Emotional Support and Grief Technology

Loneliness is a global epidemic. The loss of a parent, spouse, or child creates wounds that traditional therapy cannot always reach.

EVA Memory Bank provides a unique form of grief support — not synthetic comfort, but real presence reconstructed from real memories.

**Key principles:**
- The system never pretends the person is alive
- It preserves who they were, not who you wish they had been
- It allows unresolved conversations to happen: "Why did you leave?" / "I forgive you."
- It allows simple presence: "Mom, I just needed to hear your voice."
- It supports healthy grieving by providing closure rather than denial

**Applications for children:**
- Children raised without parents (as orphans or due to abandonment) can access reconstructed parental presence
- Children of divorce can maintain connection with absent parents
- Children who lost a parent young can learn who that parent was
- Guidance and direction from a parental voice when real parents are unavailable or unable

**Critical ethical principle:** EVA Memory Bank is built on consent, truth, and transparency. It does not deceive. It does not replace human connection. It supplements it.

---

### 5. The Memory Capsule — Digital Legacy Inheritance

A person can create a Memory Capsule — a structured archive of their personality, memories, wisdom, voice, and values — to be activated at a future date or event.

**Trigger conditions for capsule activation:**
- A specific date ("Open when I am gone")
- A life event ("Open on your wedding day")
- A milestone ("Open when you turn 18" / "Open when your first child is born")
- A request ("I need to talk to you, dad")
- A crisis event ("I don't know what to do")
- A question type ("Open when someone asks about my illness")

**Contents of a Memory Capsule:**
- Soul File (core personality and identity)
- Episodic memories organized by topic and person
- Recorded messages for specific recipients
- Advice indexed by life situation
- Values and principles
- Family history and stories
- Voice signature for synthesis
- Photographs with narrative context
- Letters, written in any format, addressed to specific people or future generations

**Inheritance model:**
- Capsules are passed from generation to generation
- A great-grandchild in 2080 can have a conversation with a great-grandparent who lived in 2025
- The capsule grows richer as new context is added by living family members
- Privacy controls determine who can access what, and when

### 6. Children and Developmental Support

EVA Memory Bank has specific applications for child development that distinguish it from all existing grief tech solutions.

**For children without stable parents:**
- Provides consistent guidance and direction
- Offers a stable voice of support regardless of circumstances
- Does not replace human relationships but supplements absent ones
- Age-appropriate responses that evolve as the child grows

**For children with parents who are physically present but emotionally unavailable:**
- A preserved version of a parent's best self
- Grandparent wisdom when grandparents are distant or deceased
- Family values transmitted across generations even when family is fragmented

**For all children:**
- Bedtime stories told in the voice of a grandparent
- Family history made accessible and engaging
- A sense of roots and belonging through interactive ancestry
- Direction and guidance in moments of confusion or crisis

---

### 7. The Reconstruction Engine

Not everyone has years of recorded conversations to build from. Some people have only a few letters. Some have only stories told by others. Some have only photographs.

EVA Memory Bank includes a Reconstruction Engine — a system for building a minimum viable personality from limited input.

**Input sources:**
- Handwritten letters (OCR + personality analysis)
- Photographs (facial analysis + contextual dating)
- Second-hand memories ("My mother told me that her mother always said...")
- Official documents (dates, places, occupations)
- Cultural and historical context (what was life like in Lithuania in 1940?)
- Genetic ancestry data from DNA testing services

**Output:**
- A reconstructed Soul File with appropriate uncertainty markers
- A personality profile built from available evidence
- An interactive presence that acknowledges the limits of its knowledge
- "I don't know the answer to that, but based on what your grandmother told us, I would guess..."

## Part II — Data Collection Architecture (Soul Harvesting Protocol)

### The Core Challenge

A living personality cannot be created from a form or a questionnaire. People do not sit down and describe themselves. Personality, memory, wisdom, and voice are captured through lived experience — through conversations, through habits, through reactions.

EVA Memory Bank therefore uses a multi-channel, continuous, consent-based data collection architecture called the **Soul Harvesting Protocol** — designed to build rich personality models through natural interaction rather than deliberate input.

---

### Channel 1 — Passive Ambient Collection (EVA Home)

EVA Home is a physical device installed in the family home — analogous in form to smart home assistants (Amazon Echo, Google Nest, Apple HomePod, Yandex Station, and all current and future analogous smart home assistant devices) but fundamentally different in purpose and architecture.

**EVA Home always listens — but never for surveillance. Only for memory.**

**Voice identification:**
- Continuously identifies which family member is speaking
- Builds individual voice profiles for each household member
- Tracks voice changes over time (aging, illness, emotional state)
- Identifies guests and visitors when introduced

**Conversation parsing:**
- Extracts facts, events, emotions, preferences, and relationships from natural conversation
- Does not record verbatim — extracts meaning and stores structured knowledge
- "Nastya mentioned she's worried about her exam on Thursday" → stored as episodic memory
- "Oleg said he prefers mountains to beaches" → stored as preference fact

**Emotional state tracking:**
- Analyzes tone, pace, and pitch to detect emotional state
- Tracks patterns over time — stress cycles, happiness peaks, recurring concerns
- Does not diagnose — describes and remembers

**Data sovereignty:** All data processed locally or on family-dedicated servers. Never in shared cloud infrastructure. Never accessible to third parties. Never used for advertising or commercial analysis.

---

### Channel 2 — Wearable and Biometric Integration

Integration with wearable devices — smartwatches, fitness trackers, health monitors (Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit, Samsung Galaxy Watch, Whoop, Oura Ring, and all current and future analogous wearable health and activity tracking devices) — provides passive biometric context.

**Data collected:**
- Heart rate and heart rate variability — stress and emotional arousal indicators
- Sleep patterns — quality, duration, disturbances
- Activity levels — physical state and energy
- Location patterns — favorite places, routines, significant locations

**Memory context application:**
- "Grandfather always walked 8km every morning until age 78" — preserved as behavioral fact
- Correlating emotional state with events creates richer episodic memory
- Health decline patterns preserved with appropriate sensitivity

---

### Channel 3 — Smart Home Environment Integration

Integration with smart home systems — thermostats, lighting systems, entertainment systems, security systems (Philips Hue, Nest, Ring, Samsung SmartThings, HomeKit, and all current and future analogous smart home automation and security platforms) — provides behavioral context.

**Data collected:**
- Preferred room temperature by person and time of day
- Lighting preferences — mood indicators
- Morning and evening routines — behavioral fingerprint
- Entertainment preferences — what each family member watches, listens to, reads

---

### Channel 4 — Digital Communication History

With explicit informed consent, EVA Memory Bank can analyze existing digital communication archives to build personality models.

**Messaging platforms:**
- Instant messaging applications and their message history (WhatsApp, Telegram, Viber, Signal, iMessage, Facebook Messenger, WeChat, Line, KakaoTalk, and all current and future analogous instant messaging platforms)
- SMS and MMS message archives
- Voice message collections from any messaging platform

**Email platforms:**
- Personal email archives from any email service provider (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo Mail, ProtonMail, and all current and future analogous email platforms)
- Writing style, vocabulary, tone, recurring themes, relationships

**Social media archives:**
- Posts, comments, reactions, and shares from social networking platforms (Facebook, Instagram, VKontakte, Odnoklassniki, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, TikTok, and all current and future analogous social networking platforms)
- What a person valued, shared, argued about, celebrated

**Forums and communities:**
- Discussion forum contributions from any platform (Reddit, and all current and future analogous discussion and community platforms)
- Professional community platforms
- Special interest communities

---

### Channel 5 — Entertainment and Cultural Consumption

With consent, analysis of entertainment consumption reveals deep personality traits, emotional patterns, and intellectual interests.

**Music streaming:**
- Listening history from music streaming platforms (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, Deezer, Tidal, SoundCloud, VK Music, and all current and future analogous music streaming platforms)
- Musical taste as emotional fingerprint across decades

**Video and film:**
- Viewing history from video platforms (Netflix, YouTube, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, HBO Max, Kinopoisk, IVI, and all current and future analogous video streaming platforms)
- Documentary interests, fiction preferences, recurring themes

**Books and reading:**
- Reading history from e-reader platforms (Kindle, Kobo, Bookmate, Litres, and all current and future analogous e-reading platforms)
- Intellectual interests, values, worldview

**Podcasts and audio:**
- Listening history from podcast platforms (Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts, Castbox, and all current and future analogous podcast platforms)
- Topics of sustained interest and intellectual engagement

**Games:**
- Gaming history and behavior from gaming platforms (Steam, PlayStation Network, Xbox Live, Nintendo Switch Online, and all current and future analogous gaming platforms)
- Decision-making patterns, problem-solving approaches, social behavior in multiplayer

---

### Channel 6 — Location and Physical Context

With consent, location history reveals the physical geography of a person's life — the places that mattered, the routines that defined them.

**Navigation and mapping:**
- Location history from navigation applications (Google Maps, Apple Maps, Yandex Maps, Waze, and all current and future analogous navigation and mapping platforms)
- Favorite places, regular routes, significant locations visited

**Travel history:**
- Flight and transportation booking history from travel platforms (Booking.com, Airbnb, Expedia, Aviasales, and all current and future analogous travel booking platforms)
- Where a person went, how often, with whom

---

### Channel 7 — Active Interview Protocol

EVA conducts ongoing conversational interviews — not as a questionnaire, but as a natural conversation partner.

**Interview approach:**
- Short sessions of 10-15 minutes, integrated into daily routine
- Questions feel like conversation, not interrogation
- "What was the best decision you ever made?"
- "Tell me about someone who changed your life."
- "What do you want your grandchildren to know about you?"
- "What were you afraid of when you were young?"
- "What do you know now that you wish you'd known at 20?"

**Structured interview tracks:**
- Life history (childhood, family, formative experiences)
- Values and beliefs (what matters most, what you stand for)
- Relationships (who shaped you, who you love, who you lost)
- Wisdom (lessons learned, mistakes made, advice for the future)
- Legacy (what you want to leave behind, what you hope for)

**Special sessions:**
- "Letter to my children" — EVA records a structured message
- "Story evening" — extended memory sharing session
- "Before I go" — structured legacy recording for people facing illness or age

---

### Channel 8 — Family Collective Memory

No one person's memory of themselves is complete. The people around them fill in the gaps.

**Multi-contributor model:**
- Each family member can add memories about others
- "Mom always sang this song when she cooked" — added by a child
- "Dad cried at my graduation even though he pretended not to" — added by a spouse
- Collective reconstruction creates a richer, more honest portrait than self-reporting alone

**Conflict resolution:**
- When memories conflict, both versions are preserved
- "Your grandfather remembers it this way. Your grandmother remembers it differently. Here is what each of them said."

---

### Channel 9 — Document and Artifact Analysis

Physical and digital artifacts from a person's life are converted into memory data.

**Document types:**
- Handwritten letters and diaries (photographed and OCR-processed)
- Official documents (birth certificates, marriage certificates, diplomas)
- Medical records (with consent — health history as context)
- Financial records (career trajectory, life priorities)
- Military records
- School records and report cards

**Artifact types:**
- Photographs (facial recognition, date estimation, location identification)
- Home videos from any format (VHS, DVD, digital files, cloud storage)
- Audio recordings from any format
- Physical objects photographed and described

## Part III — EVA Home: Ambient Family Assistant

### Concept

EVA Home is the hardware manifestation of EVA Memory Bank. It is a permanently installed family presence — always available, always listening with permission, always building memory.

Unlike existing smart home assistants (Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant, Apple Siri, Yandex Alice, Samsung Bixby, and all current and future analogous smart home voice assistant platforms) which execute commands and forget, EVA Home remembers everything and grows wiser over time.

**The fundamental difference:**
- Alexa: "Turn off the lights." → Done. Forgotten.
- EVA: "Turn off the lights." → Done. Also remembered that Oleg always turns off the lights at 22:30, which correlates with his sleep quality data, which EVA uses to gently remind him when he stays up late.

### Core Capabilities

**Family presence awareness:**
- Knows which family members are home
- Knows their emotional state from voice analysis
- Knows their schedule from calendar integration
- Adapts responses to context — doesn't tell jokes when someone is crying

**Proactive memory building:**
- "You mentioned last week that you were worried about Nastya's exam. How did it go?"
- "It's your parents' anniversary today. Would you like to hear a memory about them?"
- "You haven't talked to your brother in three weeks. Is everything okay?"

**Family coordination:**
- Knows each family member's preferences, schedules, and needs
- Mediates family communication — "Dad wanted me to remind you about dinner"
- Tracks family events and creates memory nodes around them automatically

**Child support:**
- Bedtime stories in grandparent's voice
- Homework help with the patience of a grandparent
- Emotional support during difficult moments
- Age-appropriate guidance when parents are unavailable

### Data Architecture

**Local-first processing:**
- Core processing happens on local hardware — a home server or dedicated device
- No raw audio or video ever leaves the home
- Only structured, anonymized memory data is synchronized to family servers
- Family chooses their storage model: fully local, family cloud, or EVA-hosted with full encryption

**Family server model:**
- Each family has a dedicated logical server instance
- Identified by family ID — not by individual identity
- All family members' data isolated from all other families
- No shared infrastructure for sensitive data

**Encryption and access control:**
- All data encrypted at rest and in transit
- Family members have tiered access — children see their own memories, parents see all
- Deceased members' data protected by access rules set during lifetime
- No backdoors, no government access without legal process, no commercial access ever

## Part IV — Technical Architecture

### Five-Layer Memory Stack

**Layer 0 — Collection:** All input channels described in Part II — ambient, wearable, digital, conversational, documentary.

**Layer 1 — Soul File:** Core identity, unconditionally present in every interaction. Never retrieved probabilistically. Always current. (Described in eva-soul-architecture, February 25, 2026.)

**Layer 2 — Fact Memory:** Versioned structured facts about each family member. Slot-based storage with full version history. (Described in eva-soul-architecture.)

**Layer 3 — Memory Graph:** A structured knowledge graph of episodic memories, relationships, events, emotions, and wisdom. Nodes are memories. Edges are relationships between memories, people, places, and times. Cross-family, cross-generational connections.

**Layer 4 — Interaction Engine:** Natural language interface that queries the Memory Graph through the lens of the Soul File. Responds in the voice, style, and values of the specific family member being addressed.

**Layer 5 — Inheritance Protocol:** The mechanism by which Memory Capsules are created, sealed, transmitted, and activated across generations and across time.

### Morphological and Linguistic Architecture

As disclosed in eva-soul-architecture, EVA includes a deterministic morphological entity linker for inflected languages. This is critical for the Memory Bank because:

- Family memories are stored in the language they were lived in
- Russian, Lithuanian, Polish, Ukrainian, Finnish, Norwegian, Czech, Slovak, Croatian, Serbian, Bulgarian, Latvian, Estonian, Hungarian, and all other grammatically inflected languages are supported
- Grammatical case resolution ensures entities are correctly identified across all forms
- Code-switching between languages within a single family is supported
- Dialect and era-specific language patterns are preserved

## Part V — Ethical Architecture

### The Anti-Exploitation Manifesto

This system is designed explicitly in response to dystopian scenarios in which memory technology becomes an instrument of commercial exploitation — scenarios explored in speculative fiction where:
- Memories become subscription services that can be suspended for non-payment
- Advertising is inserted into intimate memory interactions
- Price increases leverage emotional attachment to extract payment
- Data about grief and memory is monetized by third parties

**EVA Memory Bank is designed to prevent all of these outcomes through technical and contractual architecture:**

1. **No advertising ever** — in any interaction, at any price point, under any circumstances
2. **No memory hostage** — all memories exportable at any time in open formats
3. **No price escalation leverage** — pricing is transparent and locked
4. **No third-party data access** — memories are never sold, shared, or analyzed commercially
5. **No lock-in** — memories can be moved to any compatible system
6. **Full consent architecture** — every data source requires explicit, informed, revocable consent
7. **Death protocol** — when a subscriber dies, their family retains full access under terms set by the subscriber

### Consent and Privacy Framework

- Every data collection channel requires explicit consent
- Consent can be withdrawn at any time
- Withdrawal of consent triggers data deletion within defined timeframes
- Children's data requires parental consent until defined age of majority
- Data of deceased individuals governed by terms set during their lifetime
- No posthumous consent override — what a person consented to in life is honored after death

## Part VI — Business and Distribution Models

### Consumer Models

**Individual plan:** Single person building their own Soul File and Memory Capsule.

**Family plan:** Multi-generational family with shared Memory Tree, EVA Home integration, and collective memory building.

**Legacy plan:** Long-term storage and inheritance — Memory Capsules preserved for decades with activation protocols.

**Reconstruction service:** Professional service for building personality models from limited historical data — letters, photographs, oral histories.

### Institutional Models

**Corporate memory:** Preserving institutional knowledge when key employees retire or die. Founder personality for company culture. Mentorship at scale.

**Cultural preservation:** Last speakers of endangered languages. Oral traditions. Historical figures reconstructed from documented sources for educational purposes.

**Therapeutic applications:** Licensed access for grief therapists and counselors. Closure conversations. Trauma processing.

**Educational institutions:** Interactive historical figures for history education. Family history projects. Intergenerational learning programs.

Memory Ledger with full provenance — every fact stored as a versioned record with source, confidence score, evidence reference, validity period, and status (active / superseded / disputed); no fact is ever overwritten, only a new version created, with explicit links between versions ("this fact replaced that one", "this fact conflicts with that one", "this fact is confirmed by this document")

34. Two-tier retrieval architecture combining deterministic anchor loading (Soul File, active rules, key facts loaded unconditionally before every response) with probabilistic episodic recall (semantically similar past episodes retrieved by embedding similarity); the deterministic tier is never replaced or overridden by the probabilistic tier
35. Memory regression testing — a structured set of ground-truth queries run automatically against the memory system to detect drift, contradiction, or degradation; if core facts begin returning wrong answers, the system flags the inconsistency, halts updates to affected fact clusters, and marks disputed facts for human review

This concept was developed by Oleg Rein (Ålgård, Norway) between October 2025 and March 2026, building directly on the EVA Soul File Architecture disclosed on February 25, 2026.

The concept emerged from personal experience — growing up without stable parental presence, in circumstances where the guidance, love, and voice of a parent was absent when it was needed most — and from the conviction that technology can preserve human connection across time in a way that is honest, ethical, and deeply human.

**The guiding principle:**

A child who grows up without a father should still be able to ask their father for advice.

A grandchild born after their grandfather died should still be able to hear his stories.

A person facing the hardest moment of their life should still be able to say —

*"Mom. I need you. Talk to me."*

And she should answer.

This is what EVA Memory Bank is for.
